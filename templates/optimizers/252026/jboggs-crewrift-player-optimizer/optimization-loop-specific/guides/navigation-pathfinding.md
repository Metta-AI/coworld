# Navigation & pathfinding — guide (loop tier)

Reference notes, design rationale, and negative results (26).

#### 1. Fix wedging at the source (clearance + replan), not with a stuck-detector
`loop` · **negative result**

A stuck-detector is a bandaid, not a root-cause fix, and aggressive stuck-recovery can hurt score (clearance-aware pathing dropped a worst wedge from 1167 ticks permanent to ~96 ticks self-resolving). Root cause in a string-pulling planner + bang-bang axis-aligned follower (e.g. crewborg's d-pad follower): a segment counts clear if it merely grazes a corner (zero clearance) and the follower staircases off any non-axis line, so lateral drift on a wall-hugging segment hits the wall, and replanning only on goal change presses into it forever. Two fixes: (A) clearance-aware pathing -- build an ERODED mask where a pixel is clear iff its (2r+1)^2 box is fully walkable (expand walls by the agent half-width), place nodes and string-pull down corridor CENTERS on the eroded mask, but keep edges and the reachability flood on the TRUE un-eroded mask so tight passages stay reachable; (B) periodic re-planning for static-goal intents every ~4-10 ticks. Diagnose with the held-input trace: a constant diagonal for hundreds of frames = pressing into a wall.

#### 2. Stuck-recovery firing is not working; invest in avoidance and cross-check stats
`loop`

Stuck-recovery FIRING is not the same as WORKING: an agent spending 26% of an episode stuck despite a 120-tick detection window means recovery fired but never escaped, typically because it is trapped in an enemy-territory pocket where every reachable target is gated behind enemy cells, so the repick keeps choosing unreachable goals. Stuck AVOIDANCE beats stuck RECOVERY -- top competitive players basically never get stuck (best tournament max_stuck ~14 vs our agents at 2500-5875), so invest in not entering traps. Recovery refinement is also high-value only up to a point: every refinement delivered a measurable self-play A/B win (5+ score total), but once recovery is good enough the agent's dominant failure mode shifts from being-stuck to being-KILLED and further stuck work delivers ~half its predicted value, so re-check which failure mode currently dominates (replay + stuck-tick metrics) before investing more in an already-saturated mechanism. Conversely, a high max_steps_without_motion (e.g. 3529, 35% of episode) under A* navigation does NOT necessarily mean stuck-detection is broken: it can mean agents are dead/respawning or wall-impeded in unfamiliar enemy territory, so cross-check against the move-failure rate (e.g. 19%, ~1931 failed moves/agent) before concluding recovery failed.

#### 3. Landmark-first localization; cross-validate before writing the map
`loop` · **negative result**

Landmark-first localization for an egocentric observation-window game agent: scan the observation for objects whose absolute position is known a priori and back out the agent's world position (if a known landmark appears at egocentric offset (er,ec) in an R-radius window, absolute pos = known_pos - (er-R, ec-R)); since agents typically spawn adjacent to fixed structure, localization should resolve on tick 1. Where the engine renders the agent at screen center (as Coworld does), the landmark's screen position alone gives world coordinates — do NOT pixel-match. For screen-readers that DO match pixels, seed the search near the previous camera and spiral from a good seed; a naive full-frame top-left scan every frame is a severe CPU sink. Fixed structures (e.g. a hub/stations) are the source of truth and win when visible: if they imply a different position, correct spatial memory to the landmark-derived position BEFORE writing observed cells into the global map, or an unvalidated/dead-reckoned estimate smears a frame of phantom impassable objects that block A* on a navigable map. Treat hard anchors as truth, allow a single safe landmark to correct during startup but require multiple agreeing landmarks (or a hard anchor) for later corrections; do NOT add procedural/seed-specific objects as landmarks. Dead reckoning must use the engine-APPLIED action (decoded from the executed last-action displacement token), not the action the policy emitted; prefer drift-free ground-truth displacement over self-integrated position. NEGATIVE RESULT: zero-valued displacement tokens may be omitted at spawn, so a spawn-relative anchor never initializes from spawn alone — the anchor must ALSO refresh when a later landmark observation agrees with current position ('anchor learned late' is a distinct failure from 'anchor never learned').

#### 4. Bake navigation data offline from the authoritative geometry source
`loop` · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

Building a nav graph on tick 1 (flood-fill plus per-pixel node/edge over a large walkability mask plus an O(anchors^2) A* substrate) can cost many seconds on a fractional-core prod host, stalling the first frame and blowing the per-tick budget (one case: ~14s over ~814k pixels on a 1/4-core host). Bake the map data and full nav graph once into a checked-in serialized asset (nodes id/x/y/kind/room/label plus edges; an offline A* baker walks the walk mask and emits paths; ship it in the player image, e.g. as .npz), load it at startup, and treat the streamed walkability mask as a dimension/contract check, not a build input. Bake from the SAME walkability source the server uses (semantic resource rectangles are NOT the pixel-level art-layer mask; the wrong source silently produces a graph that disagrees with the live sim); validate the streamed mask against the asset at runtime and fall back to a live build on mismatch so correctness never depends on the asset.

#### 5. Use the right navigator per target regime and audit each independently
`loop` · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

A policy with multiple navigators computes each goal independently and they can diverge, so audit each separately and reproduce a bug against the regime that triggers it. In alpha_cog pursue_target, A* is used ONLY for REMEMBERED (out-of-view) targets (gated by args.astar_navigation); VISIBLE targets use BFS within the 13x13 ego window (bfs_to_visible_target) or direct adjacency, which is sufficient and cheaper -- do not unify the two blindly. In the transit-through-interactive bug the BFS and greedy navigators correctly used the target's FREE NEIGHBORS as goal; only the A* navigator pathed to the target cell itself and was buggy, so the bug lived in only the remembered/out-of-view regime.

#### 6. Lookahead steering must be wall-aware and direction-correct
`loop` · **negative result** · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

NEGATIVE RESULT: pure-pursuit/lookahead steering (advance N pixels along the baked path, steer straight at that point) drives the agent into walls when the path bends sharply, because the lookahead target lands on the far side of a wall; even bake-time simplification does not guarantee the straight line between two retained waypoints is traversable. Lookahead distance is a tradeoff (smaller is safer near tight geometry, sluggish in open space), so prefer ADAPTIVE lookahead or wall-aware target selection over one global constant. Separately, a monotonic-only progress variable (pathProgress = max(pathProgress, snapIdx)) is a trap on REVERSIBLE edges: if the path is stored in fixed source->destination order, traversing it in reverse snaps progress near the far end and pins the bot toward the wrong end -- directional path data needs a direction parameter and reverse-indexing, not just clamping progress upward.
  <sub>sources: opencode:ses_1fb1d9989ffeYXiew23WUqo2hw, opencode:ses_201972733ffexPIP9mq6yEDpqJ</sub>

#### 7. Prefer target-selection avoidance over per-tick nav avoidance (except full-plan A*)
`loop` · **negative result**

NEGATIVE RESULT: prefer target-selection-level avoidance (changing WHERE agents go) over per-tick navigation-level avoidance (changing HOW they get there) for danger/territory -- filtering targets in enemy territory shipped a horizon-scaling win, whereas per-tick territory-aware greedy nav and full-4-direction BFS both regressed at 10k with huge per-seed variance (E035 full-BFS: +0.124 at 2k but -0.315 at 10k; +5.54 on seed 256, -11.76 on seed 7). Root cause: changing even one agent's intermediate path shifts which cells teammates observe, cascading position changes that break the aligner cycle, and the biased-BFS failure mode is load-bearing for downstream flags. Gate any per-tick nav change to confirmed no-progress episodes. CRITICAL CAVEAT: target-selection avoidance does NOT prevent A* routing THROUGH enemy cells; alpha_cog's avoid_enemy_territory_radius filters only selection, so once A* plots through enemy territory the agent walks into the -1 HP/tick zone -- in-transit safety needs a separate finite-A*-penalty mechanism (which is the one per-tick exception that composes, since A* commits a full plan).

#### 8. Localize sparingly, never re-validate per tick, and treat a wrong map as worse than none
`loop` · **negative result** · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

Localize sparingly: only on the first tick and after death, using dead-reckoning between those events; re-localizing every tick yields wildly wrong positions once the agent leaves spawn. NEGATIVE RESULT: continuous per-step localization cross-validation inside the map update() path itself corrupts the map -- remove it and expose an explicit reset_localization() that clears position/localized flag on a declarative trigger (e.g. agent-death detection) rather than re-validating every tick. Treat a wrong map as worse than no map (all downstream decisions build on it): prefer leaving cells unknown over guessing them, and known-static cells (walls, stations, extractors from direct observation or the fixed spawn layout) must NEVER be overwritten by later 'empty' observations. Position accuracy is load-bearing -- A* starts from spatial.position, so a wrong position yields a path from a ghost cell with a wrong first step.

#### 9. Diagnose a wedge before fixing; test the metric the follower actually uses
`loop` · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

A crewmate standing still at a task until killed is almost always a navigation wedge, not a completion bug -- distinguish via the held-button mask in traces (mask 32 = holding A, actually doing a task; a constant diagonal like 9 = pressing into a wall), and remember the viewer's PlayerN labels do NOT map to your connection slots so scan all slots. #### 10. Suspect one motion-layer root cause for paired symptoms; never overload a tuning constant
`loop` · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

A single broken velocity/motion estimate can produce two distinct symptoms at once: orbiting/overshooting targets (no velocity-based hysteresis) AND sticking on wall corners (the stuck-detector never sees motion change). When both appear together, suspect ONE root cause in the motion/velocity layer rather than two bugs. Relatedly, do not overload one tuning constant: a single CLOSE_DISTANCE used as both the movement arrival deadband and the on-top-of-target interact-proximity check cannot satisfy both -- movement needs a LOOSER deadband (~16-20px) to survive momentum while interaction needs to stay TIGHT (~12px). Split into ARRIVAL_DEADBAND vs CLOSE_DISTANCE before tuning either.

#### 11. Dead-reckon position from the engine's EXECUTED action, never second-guess success
`loop` · **negative result**

Maintain position by dead-reckoning from the engine-reported APPLIED action token (the executed last-action plus its move flag), NOT the action the policy emitted, because the engine may reject or alter it. Position-tracking drift is commonly caused by SECOND-GUESSING the last action: do NOT re-derive whether a move succeeded — if the executed action reports a move, update unconditionally because the simulator already validated it; re-deciding diverges your estimate from the authoritative result and accumulates drift. Internal position lags the sim by exactly one step (at step N you read step N-1's executed action), so verify with internal_delta[N] == action_delta[N-1], not coincidence. This also keeps tracking correct when a human takes over. Scripted policies often track RELATIVE to their own origin (e.g. centered (100,100)) while the sim uses ABSOLUTE coordinates, so when diagnosing coordinate bugs compare DELTAS, never raw values.

#### 12. Diagnose movement bugs with the action histogram, layer bisection, and a sibling oracle
`loop`

When improving a scripted policy, most of the win is in HOW the agent moves and WHAT it targets, not goal selection -- a good goal picker that oscillates, freezes, or wanders never realizes its plan, so diagnose movement/targeting from the WORST episodes first and map each symptom (frozen, A->B->A->B looping, piling on a hub, wandering, dying, head-on collisions) to a module and knob rather than guessing parameters. Use the per-episode action histogram as a cheap health metric (no replay): healthy alpha_cog ~30% noop with balanced movement; broken run 75-80% noop; a symmetric/uniform distribution across opposing directions plus self-position cycling through a tight ~12px loop is the smoking gun for a path-follow ORBIT (distinct from wall-stuck); but allowlist benign single-frame mask=0 blips and the pre-game noop run by segmenting per phase. When intent looks right but movement is wrong, bisect layers explicitly: confirm the A* corridor, then intent direction, then the intent->action-index->button-mask mapping is byte-for-byte identical between policy and server protocol, only then suspect path-following. A working SIBLING mode sharing the same encoding pipeline is a diagnostic oracle: if it moves fluidly, the shared pipeline is ruled out and the bug is in the broken mode's logic (e.g. imposter mode had a 240-tick followee swap floor that crewmate mode lacked -- the missing commitment was the bug). And always verify the hypothesized fix could even FIRE on the actual trace before building it (a miner's missing-gear was an exploration-coverage gap, so a memory-cache fix could never have fired).

#### 13. Debug a frozen or GET_GEAR-stuck cogsguard agent at the right layer
`loop`

Debug a frozen or acquisition-stuck scripted game agent at the right layer. A frozen agent (same position over many steps) is usually a blocking wall/obstacle, a using-object flag suppressing the position update, or low energy DELAYING (not failing) the action -- distinguish by comparing the executed last action against the intended last action for mismatches. When an agent never leaves an early acquisition phase (e.g. cogsguard never leaves GET_GEAR / never reaches execute_role), the cause is usually that exploration never reaches the needed station, or the station isn't discovered due to a wrong object-name pattern; fix in the policy's exploration/discovery routines (e.g. _explore / _update_occupancy_and_discover), not the role logic. A draw-by-timeout with zero objectives signals the policy is STRUCTURALLY stuck (a navigation or trigger failure), not losing -- suspect nav before tuning strategy weights.

#### 14. Use a territory projection layer for selection but keep the live sensor authoritative for safety
`loop`

Compute a per-cell territory PROJECTION layer from last-known alignments (friendly hub strength 20, connected friendly junctions 10, connected enemy junctions/known enemy units 10, contested project both, disconnected project nothing; influence decays by Euclidean distance; ties resolve neutral). Use this projection ONLY for target selection (e.g. nearest safe retreat) and keep the directly-observed current-cell territory sensor AUTHORITATIVE for mode-exit safety checks; when the live sensor says the current cell is unsafe, make the retreat helper ignore nearby STALE projected-friendly cells and prefer a farther remembered safe cell so the agent doesn't oscillate on a locally-remembered-friendly cell while HP drains. A safety filter applied in ONE selection path is not automatically global: in alpha_cog the _in_danger filter covers the visible-target path but is ABSENT from the remembered-target non-frontier path, so miners get unfiltered targets -- verify safety coverage per code path and per role, and distinguish a SELECTION-time territory filter from a separate EXECUTION-time _path_is_survivable (H085) HP-aware check before adding redundant filtering. Cogs-vs-Clips junctions are only alignable within network range (15 cells of any team net:-tagged object OR 25 cells of the hub), so derive capturability from a private per-agent connectivity graph using those exact ranges, not a loose nearest-junction heuristic.

#### 15. Track unseen Crewrift agents with a reachability disc and deterministic path-mixture belief
`loop`

Build a multi-agent position filter that is cheap, deterministic, and RNG-free by reframing the only real latents as (i) which tasks each agent is assigned and (ii) which it's heading to; given a destination the A* path and position are deterministic from the static map, so represent belief as a soft task-assignment distribution plus a destination mixture rolled forward along PRECOMPUTED A* polylines (no live search, no Monte-Carlo -- spread comes from the mixture, not sampling). Bound an unseen agent with a hard reachability disc: T ticks after last sighting it is within speed*T pixels (Crewrift ~2.75 px/tick), intersected with reachable cells -- exact, cheap, assumption-free, so use it as the backbone before any behavioral prior. Sharpen the destination posterior WHILE the agent is still in view by down-weighting hypotheses whose predicted position diverges from observed; when a hypothesis's ETA passes, decay its mass to uniform-within-the-disc. Size the occupancy grid (~32px bins) to the EXPECTED path-prediction divergence, not arbitrarily fine, since polylines only approximate real paths and pixel-exact prediction would be falsely confident; the grid doubles as the error model and gets corridors for free. Reuse this static substrate: per-anchor-to-button route polylines can be sampled for front-biased loiter points, so 'front of the ship' is just proximity-to-button-anchor plus those corridors.

#### 16. Check what the environment hands you for free before building search; and beware shared-action determinism
`loop`

Before building search/exploration for a game policy, check what GLOBAL information the environment already gives for free -- if a minimap already reveals the whole map and other agents' locations, an explore task is pure cost and should be dropped entirely. Most game states (lobby, role reveal, gameplay, deaths) are reachable WITHOUT navigation; only a few (e.g. voting at a button/body) need it, and navigation-to-trigger-a-view is a chicken-and-egg trap worth avoiding when a state is otherwise reachable. Also remember that in a deterministic multi-agent simulator the same single-agent action does NOT guarantee the same post-step state: the outcome depends on the FULL action vector across all agents, so teammates at different cells resolve collisions differently -- when diffing two traces for divergence, check teammate positions and the full observation window at the divergence tick, not just the focal agent's action.

#### 17. Treat base navigation/BFS as the highest-risk change; capture upside via an adjacent low-coupling mechanism
`loop`

When an optimization axis is high-leverage (largest per-seed score swings) but cascade-prone because it is tightly coupled with existing logic, look for an ADJACENT lower-coupling mechanism that captures the upside without rewriting the core. In alpha_cog, direct navigation/BFS changes never shipped because they cascade through the entire shipped flag stack, but a navigation-ADJACENT mechanism (stuck detection/recovery) shipped as V7 and beyond. Treat any change to base navigation/BFS as the highest-risk class. Pre-gear early-game routing using BFS within only the limited observation window is especially fragile: a teammate whose position differs between runs can block the path and cascade into the agent roaming hundreds of ticks without ever equipping, so reason about second-order effects on OTHER agents' observation windows, not just the flagged agent.

#### 19. Rank repair candidates by local reachability; leash HP at the mode level
`loop` · **negative result** · ⚠ _session-derived, unverified_

Rank frontier repair/rebuild target candidates by LOCAL reachability, not global graph importance: repeated graph-priority attempts regressed because they pulled agents toward globally high-value but locally expensive bridge targets (sometimes effectively inside Clips space), so order candidates by travel distance first, then local chain reconnection/support, deliberately NOT by global bridge value, preferring nearby capturable neutral junctions that reconnect a local chain on the friendly side. #### 20. Model the cogs-vs-clips energy/HP economy; energy-failed moves look like walls
`loop` · _see related: S018 (other tier)_

Energy and HP are active mechanics a nav policy must respect. Movement costs 4 energy/step; a day/night cycle (day_length 200) grants solar +3/tick day, +1/night; FRIENDLY territory regen (+100/tick plus solar) far exceeds the base energy cap of 20, so agents in territory are always full and move freely, while OUTSIDE territory they move only ~every 1-2 ticks by day and ~every 4 ticks at night. HP: base 100 (initial 50 in cogsguard), -1/tick always plus an extra -1/tick in enemy territory, healed to full in friendly territory; at HP 0 hearts and gear are cleared. avoid_enemy_territory_radius filters only TARGET selection, not in-transit pathing, so a long mission can die mid-transit and silently lose gear. CRITICAL: an energy-stalled failed move is INDISTINGUISHABLE from a wall/agent block in the move token (the sim executes noop), so a policy that ignores energy will misread exhaustion as obstruction and thrash. Rather than building heavy 'go recharge' logic, detect the failure (intended move != executed action) and RETRY up to a small cap (default 3), since agents auto-regen each tick and regen fully near aligned buildings. Trigger retreat on distance-aware vitals (should_retreat if hp<30 OR energy<8 OR hp < anchor_distance+15) and only energy needs a rest ceiling since friendly HP regen is effectively instant. Energy-aware behavior is a large untapped navigation lever: prefer targets in or near friendly territory at night, noop to REST when energy is low instead of burning failed-move ticks, and extend territory to create energy-healing corridors.

#### 21. Death loops: model damage as geometry, and blacklisting lethal cells fails
